BIAXIALALL` SYMBOL             

TrueAnglePupil2

 TrueAnglePupil2[nx_,frac_,shifta_,shiftb_,DegMax_,shiftx_:0,shifty_:0]  Generates three arrays of numbers corresponding to images of the microscope objective pupil.  Output is a vector of arrays of the form {THETAARRAY,PHIARRAY,WINDOW}.

The value at each point in THETAARRAY corresponds to the incident angle on the sample from an ray originating at that point in the exit pupil

The value at each point in PHIARRAY corresponds to the azimuthal angle on the sample from an ray originating at that point in the exit pupil

The array window is a binary array defining the aperture of the exit pupil.


Inputs are defined as follows

nx - Half the array size in pixels (For example, nx=49 generates an array size of 98 pixels)

frac - The fraction of the array that contains the pupil (for example  frac=0.80 will generate the three arrays corresponding to a pupil that fills 80% of the simulated image)

shifta - Is the angle the central ray from the microscope objective makes on the sample along the direction of an azimutal angle of 0° (ie. the x direction). This value is determined by the orientation of the sample with respect to the microscope objective optical axis.  (This value is non-zero when the sample is tilted about the rotation axis of the polarimeter arm)

shiftb - Is the angle the central ray from the microscope objective makes on the sample along the direction of an azimutal angle of 90° (ie. the y direction). This value is determined by the orientation of the sample with respect to the microscope objective optical axis. (This value is non-zero when the sample is tilted about and axis perpendicular the rotation axis of the polarimeter arm)

DegMax - This is the ArcSin of the numerical aperture of the microscope objective.

shiftx and shift y - shift the centroid of the pupil image in the array
